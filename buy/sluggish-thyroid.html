Sluggish Thyroid Triple

Iodine + Selenium + Iron

Iodine is the raw material for T3/T4. Iron activates the enzyme that incorporates iodine. Selenium converts inactive T4 into active T3. Missing any one creates a bottleneck the other two cannot compensate for.

Critical: If taking levothyroxine or any thyroid medication, iron must be taken at least 4 hours apart. Iron significantly reduces medication absorption. Excess iodine can worsen Hashimoto's. Work with your doctor on dosing for all three.

How to take this stack: Take 150-200 mcg iodine and 200 mcg selenomethionine together in the morning with food. If directed by your doctor, take 25-45 mg ferrous bisglycinate at a separate meal, at least 4 hours from thyroid medication. Selenium toxicity occurs above 400 mcg/day; do not exceed 200 mcg. Benefits accumulate over weeks to months of consistent use.
